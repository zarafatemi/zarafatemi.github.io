About me

I am a Ph.D. student in the department of Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Chicago (UIC), advised by Professor Elena Zheleva. My research interests lie at the intersection of Causal Inference, Machine Learning, and Social Network Analysis. I am particularly interested in adapting machine learning methods for 1) mitigating biases in Randomized Control Trial (RCT) or A/B testing 2) counterfactual reasoning, and 3) controlling confounding bias in network data.

I received my Master's degree in Network Science from University of Tehran, where I was advised by Professor Mostafa Salehi. During my M.Sc., I focused on proposing a pipeline to reveal and visualize hidden structures in networks.
